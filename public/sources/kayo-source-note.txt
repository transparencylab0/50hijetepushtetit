50 hijet e pushtetit - KAYO defence industry source note

Public source note for editorial review, date 2026-07-06.

Purpose

This note supports the KAYO investigation with a public-facing map of sources.
It is not a court finding and does not declare guilt. It separates primary
records, documentary reporting, media allegations, and open verification gaps.

Evidence grades used in the working file

- A: Primary record: law, VKM, QKB/OpenCorporates registry, treasury data,
  court filing, company report, or official statement.
- B: Reputable documentary reporting that cites named records, official
  statements, filings, or direct quotes.
- C: Media allegation, unnamed-source reporting, resident/opposition claim, or
  interpretive claim that must be attributed in the article.
- D: Not yet source-ready as an assertion; soften, remove, or convert into a
  public question.

Media and documentary reporting buckets

- Pamfleti / Kapitali: use for Yeffet litigation details, alleged Rama links,
  TIMAK/Idrizi network claims, alleged offshore or hidden-ownership plans, and
  reported Ministry-of-Defence source claims. Grade C unless backed by filings
  or registry records.
- Hashtag.al, VoxNews, BalkanWeb, Sot News, Timoni.al: use for treasury-flow
  reporting, MSSC/Dusha reporting, News24 eviction reporting, Kodër Babrru
  resident claims, opposition statements, and political allegations. Grade B
  where documents are named; grade C where sourcing is anonymous or adversarial.
- BIRN, OSCE, EU Delegation, RSF, SafeJournalists: use for News24/media-freedom
  condemnation and public-interest context. Grade B unless the underlying
  legal property record is attached.
- Haaretz, Follow The Money, The Defense Post, DIMSE, SeeNews, Albanian Times:
  use for international confirmation of defence-industry, Elbit, Fincantieri,
  NATO/NSPA, and regional procurement context.
- Transparency International: use for Air Albania / public-private governance
  context, not as proof of KAYO facts.

High-risk claims that need primary backup before being stated as fact

- Exact TIMAK DEFENCE treasury total and each payment date/value.
- Exact MSSC cumulative state-contract total and the proportion awarded through
  direct negotiation.
- SPAK status for MSSC, Emiljano Dusha, Dorjan Ducka, Belinda Balluku, or any
  other named person.
- Claims that tenders were rigged, that companies were used as fronts, or that
  named individuals trafficked weapons.
- The alleged Elbit sovereign-guarantee amount, interest exposure, and financing
  structure.
- The Kodër Babrru safety-buffer breach, permit description, cadastral plots,
  ownership status, and lack of consent.
- Any claim that News24 files were removed from computers or that enforcement
  occurred without a court order.
- Claims about Senegal, Togo, Miami-Dade litigation, and the "$30 million
  Albanian project" until the docket records are attached.

Publication rule

Primary-record claims can be stated directly. Media-only claims should be
phrased as attributed reporting. Unsupported assertions should become questions
or be removed from the publishable article.
