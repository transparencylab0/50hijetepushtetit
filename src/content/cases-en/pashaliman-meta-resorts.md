This file does not say the project is illegal. It says something simpler and more important: when a tourism project touches former military land, a lagoon, coastline, cultural heritage and possible state participation, the documents cannot remain in the shadows.

At the centre are facts that should be checked against source material: **Meta Resorts Albania shpk**, NUIS **M11823016C**; strategic status for **Pashaliman Laguna Eko Resort**; a declared investment of **EUR 21 million**; **78 jobs**; roughly **25 hectares** in **Pashaliman/Orikum**; company capital of **100 ALL** in extracts; and a presidential act concerning former military property before approval by the **Strategic Investment Committee**.

## Names to follow

- **Meta Resorts Albania shpk**: the company presented as applicant/investor.
- **Dante Mazzitelli**: appears in the source note as administrator and 100% owner in company extracts.
- **Strategic Investment Committee**: the institution that approves strategic status.
- **Edi Rama**: Prime Minister and chair of the SIC in decision-making.
- **Albanian Investment Corporation**: the state structure that, according to the source note, enters the project-preparation phase.
- **Presidency** and **Ministry of Defence**: institutions linked to the former military property and Decree no. 24.
- **Laura Plaku (Saro)**: mentioned in the source note as an issue requiring institutional clarification and right of reply, not as a conclusion.

## What is known so far

**Meta Resorts Albania shpk** is the entity linked to the **Pashaliman Laguna Eko Resort** project. The source note links the SIC decision to an expected investment of **EUR 21 million**, **78 jobs** and an area of about **25 hectares** in **Pashaliman/Orikum**.

The source note says the company had **100 ALL** in capital in extracts. That does not prove the project lacks money. But for a EUR 21 million project, the key document becomes proof of financing: who finances it, with what guarantee, what debt, what partners and what conditions.

The source note also mentions **Decree no. 24**, dated **03.02.2025**, for property no. 1314, the former **Southern Naval Flotilla**. That step needs light: why was the property removed from the military plan, what opinion did Defence give, was there a security assessment, and was any allied structure consulted when the area is linked to a naval base of public importance?

## What cannot yet be said

It cannot yet be said that there was a violation. It cannot yet be said that financing is missing. It cannot yet be said that the state has lost property or rights. It cannot yet be said that there is a conflict of interest. Those are exactly the reasons the documents are needed.

The point is not noise. The point is the file: the full decision, annexes, Defence opinion, AIC trace, public-asset valuation, environmental and cultural permits, financing documents and conflict-of-interest declarations.

Until those documents are public, this file remains **P1**: high priority for sources, not a conclusion.
