One name in reporting about SKY ECC. A series of charter flights with Edmond Bego. A police officer's testimony about pressure inside a station. And, above all, a question that remains open: **why has such a long public trail still not produced a full institutional answer?**

Taulant Balla is one of the most important figures in Albania's Socialist Party: MP, party leader, Interior Minister and co-chair of an EU–Albania parliamentary committee. This profile follows six trails around his power — not to declare anyone guilty, but to show what is known, what is disputed and which documents are still missing.

## In one minute

- **SKY ECC:** media have reported that Balla's name appears in communications extracted from the encrypted network; the original case record is not public in the sources reviewed.
- **Edmond Bego:** a public association that keeps returning through tenders, travel and asset investigations.
- **Charter flights:** reported counts vary — 18, 25 or 57 — while the questions about payment and disclosure remain open.
- **Fushë-Krujë:** a former police officer made a public allegation that Balla denied.
- **Toyota Yaris and ONE Albania:** two files where Balla's name or family connection entered public debate, but fact must be separated from interpretation.
- **The institutional gap:** many public questions, few accessible closing documents.

## Public role

Balla has been a Socialist Party MP for the Elbasan/Librazhd area, a leader of the Socialist parliamentary group and Interior Minister in 2024. In 2025, the EEAS identified him as co-chair of the EU–Albania Stabilisation and Association Parliamentary Committee. Those roles make transparency around business relationships, policing, justice and political finance especially important.

## Six trails worth following

### 1. Edmond Bego: the relationship that keeps returning

Edmond Bego, also known as Mond Bega, has been reported as having been convicted in Italy for participation in a criminal organisation and drug trafficking. In 2018, prosecutors announced a property investigation, while media reported on tenders and a public association with Balla.

This **does not by itself prove** that Balla committed a criminal offence. It creates verifiable questions: which contracts did Bego win, through what procedure, who paid for the travel, and what was declared in asset forms? Travel counts vary by source — 18, 25 or 57 — so they should not be converted into one “fact” without the underlying TIMS/Albcontrol data and payment records.

Balla denied the claim about 18 charter trips and said the information could be obtained from Albcontrol; another report quotes him as acknowledging that he knew the Bego family while denying that he provided political protection. Those responses should remain alongside the allegations, not be replaced by them.

### 2. SKY ECC and “File 26/1”: the most serious allegation

Follow the Money and Albanian media have reported that Balla's name appears in communications extracted from the encrypted SKY ECC network. The published claims include requests allegedly connected to the arrest of rivals of a criminal group and the securing of votes before the 2021 election.

In the sources reviewed for this profile, we did not find a public indictment or court judgment proving that narrative against Balla. The accurate formulation is therefore **“a reported allegation requiring verification against the original case record,”** not “Balla cooperated with gangs.” The official status of the file, transcript authenticity, chain of custody and any public SPAK position should be obtained.

### 3. Fushë-Krujë: one testimony, many missing records

In 2018, former police officer Emiljano Nuhu alleged that he was threatened in a police setting in the presence of officials and people connected to the Rraja family, naming Taulant Balla as well. Balla denied the allegation and called it slander.

This subject should be followed through the complaint, camera footage, SHÇBA records, prosecution decisions and any document showing how the matter ended. The reported testimony is material for further reporting, not conclusive proof of guilt.

### 4. Toyota Yaris: from seizure to political allegation

In reporting about the “Toyota Yaris” case, Balla's name has been introduced through political accusations and claims about encrypted communications. The seizure of money and the existence of proceedings must be documented separately from any claim about a politician's role. The relevant documents are the prosecution/court decisions, seizure records and the current status of the investigation.

### 5. Family assets and ONE Albania: the conflict question

Public asset-declaration data lists income declared by Balla's wife, Iris Pekmezi, from employment at Telekom Albania/ONE and from legal consulting. This is a documentary fact; it is not by itself proof of illegal compensation or a conflict of interest.

A 2026 report alleges that ONE Albania did not cooperate promptly with an investigation into call centres and connects this to Pekmezi's position. To publish that as a finding, the full court decision, police/prosecution requests, the company's responses and the official description of her role are needed. A family relationship cannot substitute for those documents.

## What the evidence still cannot say

The initial material mentions links to radical religious movements and a mosque in Balla's area. The listed sources do not establish that connection. This section should not be published as fact because it would be a serious conclusion without verifiable documents.

Likewise, the claim that Balla has “€600,000 in unexplained wealth” from charter flights is a calculation based on an assumed per-flight cost, not an audit. It should remain a question until invoices, contracts, payer and relevant declarations are located.

## 6. The institutional contradiction: European power, unresolved questions

Balla's role as SAPC co-chair places him in a position where he speaks about the rule of law and European integration. Reporting on his public attack against BIRN and journalist Gjergj Erebara raises a separate question about standards of communication with the press: does a public official answer evidence with documents, or move the debate toward funders and personal attacks?

That is a public-accountability and media-freedom question, not proof of a criminal offence.

## What would settle the questions

- The official status of File 26/1 and any public SPAK act concerning SKY ECC.
- TIMS/Albcontrol travel data, charter invoices and declarations of gifts or expenses.
- The full list of tenders won by companies connected to Edmond Bego, including procedures and contract performance.
- Full decisions on investigations into Bego and any later asset proceedings.
- SHÇBA and prosecution records concerning Emiljano Nuhu's testimony.
- Seizure records and decisions in the Toyota Yaris case.
- Original asset declarations, not only media reproductions.
- Written responses from Balla, the Socialist Party, SPAK, ONE Albania, Iris Pekmezi and other named parties.
