More than 70 vehicle-rental payments. More than 150 million **old lek**. A company founded and originally owned by Blendi Gonxhja, administered for years by his wife, Elda Gonxhja, and later transferred entirely to her.

That is the core of the controversy linking the Gonxhja family to Integrated Energy BV, the concessionaire associated with the Tirana incinerator. The payment trail has been reported by Albanian media and used by opposition politicians to call for a SPAK investigation. The public record reviewed here does not establish that the payments were illegal. It does establish a set of concrete questions about public-private relationships, disclosure and oversight.

## In one minute

- **The company:** ALBARENT is a vehicle-rental and passenger-transport business identified in QKB-derived records by NIPT K92108006A.
- **The family link:** Blendi Gonxhja was the original 100-percent owner; Elda Gonxhja was administrator from 2010 and is now listed as sole owner.
- **The reported payments:** Boldnews reported more than 70 transfers from Integrated Energy to ALBARENT between 2019 and 2021.
- **The amount:** media reports cite approximately 151 million old lek—about 15.1 million modern ALL, not 151 million modern ALL.
- **The political issue:** the payments allegedly involved a company linked to the wife of a senior public official.
- **The missing proof:** invoices, contracts, bank records, procurement files and an official finding on legality.

## What the registry confirms

ALBARENT Sh.p.k. was founded in 2009. Historical registry information identifies Blendi Gonxhja as the original 100-percent owner. Elda Gonxhja became administrator in July 2010 and later became the sole shareholder.

The company’s registered activity includes vehicle rental, rental with driver, taxi and passenger transport. QKB-derived company records report turnover of 156.8 million ALL in 2021, 160.7 million ALL in 2022, 263.6 million ALL in 2023 and 165.7 million ALL in 2024. [ALBARENT registry record](https://opencorporates.al/sq/nipt/k92108006a)

The registry also records a tax-related security measure over the company’s assets in October 2021, released in November 2023. That confirms a tax-administration action; it does not, without the underlying assessment and payment file, prove tax evasion or establish the size of any debt.

## What the payment reports say

In March 2022, Boldnews reported that Integrated Energy BV had made more than 70 payments to ALBARENT during 2019–2021 for vehicle-rental services. The report said the information was supported by documents from the tax administration. [Boldnews report](https://boldnews.al/2022/03/31/blendi-gonxhe-mori-150-mln-leke-nga-inceneratori-i-sharres-per-makina-me-qera/)

The figure was described as more than 150 million old lek. This distinction is essential: 150 million old lek equals approximately 15 million modern ALL, or roughly €125,000–€130,000. It should not be presented as €1.2 million or 150 million modern ALL.

CNA later reported that the Democratic Party asked SPAK to investigate an alleged 151-million-old-lek payment relationship between Integrated Energy and ALBARENT. The article presents a political allegation and quotes opposition claims; it is not a court finding. [CNA report](https://www.cna.al/politike/151-mln-leke-nga-integrated-energy-bv-spv-pd-kerkon-hetim-per-blendi-go-i374122)

## Why Elda Gonxhja matters to the story

The central accountability question is not simply whether ALBARENT existed or whether it rented vehicles. It is whether a company connected to the family of a public official received payments from a concessionaire whose relationship with public institutions was already politically controversial, and whether the arrangement was properly disclosed and managed.

At the time of the reported payments, Blendi Gonxhja was director of DPSHTRR. Elda Gonxhja was the company’s administrator. On 11 January 2024, shortly before Gonxhja entered the cabinet, ownership was transferred to Elda through a gift contract. The timing raises a conflict-of-interest question, but it does not by itself prove an unlawful transfer or concealment. [Company record](https://opencorporates.al/sq/nipt/k92108006a)

The 2021 asset declaration lists ALBARENT at its nominal capital of 100,000 ALL. That is the company’s registered share capital, not an independent valuation of a business reporting millions of ALL in annual turnover. Any conclusion about undervaluation would require accounting and valuation documents. [ILDKPKI declaration](https://spending.data.al/files/money_power/Blendi-Gonxhja-PVSD-2021%20%281%29.pdf)

## SPAK and the unresolved status

Gonxhja appeared at SPAK in July 2022 after a complaint by Nisma Thurje concerning alleged abuse of office and tender violations at DPSHTRR. His appearance is documented; it is not evidence of guilt. [Euronews report](https://euronews.al/blendi-gonxhe-paraqitet-ne-spak/)

Media reports have described broader allegations concerning the incinerator payments and possible conflicts of interest. In the records reviewed for this article, no official SPAK decision was found confirming a criminal charge, a final finding or the current status of a related investigation.

## What would settle the story

- The contracts between Integrated Energy and ALBARENT.
- The invoices and vehicle-delivery or usage records.
- Bank statements or Treasury records showing the actual sums paid.
- The tax-administration documents cited by Boldnews.
- The complete QKB file for the January 2024 share transfer.
- Any SPAK, GJKKO or court decision concerning the payments.
- A written response from Gonxhja, Elda Gonxhja, ALBARENT and Integrated Energy.

Until those records are published, the defensible conclusion is limited: media reports describe payments from the Tirana incinerator concessionaire to a family-linked vehicle-rental company, while registry and declaration records confirm the family connection. Whether the payments represented ordinary commercial services, an unmanaged conflict of interest or something unlawful remains an open question.
