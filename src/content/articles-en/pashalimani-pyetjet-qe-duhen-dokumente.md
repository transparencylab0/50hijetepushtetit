This is not an accusation. It is a simple way to separate what is known from what still needs documents.

In Pashaliman, the public file concerns a tourism project declared strategic, a former military area and the possibility of state participation through public structures. Those three elements are enough to make the basic documents a minimum condition for public debate, not an editorial luxury.

## What is known

From the source note and public traces, the project is linked to **Meta Resorts Albania shpk**, strategic status, a declared investment value and an area in Pashaliman/Orikum. These points should always be read next to the original document, because a summary cannot replace the full decision.

The documents that matter are the full SIC decision, annexes, Defence opinion, any public-property valuation, environmental permits and any agreement with state structures.

## What should not be said without proof

Without additional documents, it cannot be said that the project is illegal. It cannot be said that the state lost property, that financing is missing, or that there is a conflict of interest. Those are claims that require clear proof.

What can be said is narrower and more useful: when public property, security, coastline and private investment meet in one file, the documents should be readable by the public.

## Public questions

- What property, rights or access does the state provide?
- Who controls the project structure?
- What proof of financing was submitted?
- Were environmental, cultural-heritage and security opinions obtained?
- What conditions protect the public interest if the project changes, is delayed or fails?

Until those questions receive documents, the file remains open for verification.
