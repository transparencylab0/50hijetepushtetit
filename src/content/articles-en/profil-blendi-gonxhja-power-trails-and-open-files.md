The slides call him “the family man.” The public record shows a family-linked transport business, reported payments from the Tirana incinerator concessionaire, a son's declared business profit, another son's declared Netherlands apartment, a company transfer immediately before a ministerial appointment, a 2022 SPAK appearance and a 400-million-ALL state intervention for a Kanye West concert.

Blendi Gonxhja's public profile sits at the intersection of political longevity, family business and contested public spending. This is not a court judgment. It is a map of what is documented, what has been alleged and which questions still require original records.

## In one minute

- **The Rama connection:** Gonxhja has worked with Edi Rama from the Ministry of Culture to Tirana Municipality and national government.
- **ALBARENT:** company records show Blendi as original owner and Elda Gonxhja as current 100-percent owner.
- **The timing question:** ownership moved to Elda on 11 January 2024; Gonxhja was appointed minister on 12 January.
- **Incinerator-linked payments:** Boldnews reported more than 70 transfers to ALBARENT from Integrated Energy during 2019–2021; the cited figure is approximately 151 million old lek and needs primary-source verification.
- **Family assets:** declarations record Dante's 34.57 million ALL business profit in 2021 and David's €350,000 Netherlands apartment.
- **SPAK:** his appearance and a complaint are documented; the later ministerial and parliamentary promotions are documented, but no official case outcome was located.
- **Kanye West:** the government approved 400 million ALL in public support for a private concert, a decision defended by Gonxhja and challenged by critics.

## The visual dossier's thesis

The nine-slide dossier follows one argument: public money and family-linked businesses should be read together. Its strongest documented points are the company registry, ILDKPKI declarations, reported Integrated Energy payments, the January 2024 transfer and Gonxhja's public roles. Its strongest language—“his wife cashed incinerator checks,” “nothing happened” after SPAK and “paid for how?”—is editorial framing. The underlying documents support questions; they do not automatically establish a crime.

## A career built around Edi Rama

Gonxhja's political story is also a story about institutional continuity. He served as chief of cabinet at the Ministry of Culture in 1998, when Edi Rama was minister, and became deputy mayor of Tirana in 2000 while Rama was mayor. After working outside government, he returned to the Tirana administration in 2015 and later led DPSHTRR from 2018 until entering the cabinet.

His official biography also records his participation in the 1990 student movement, his 1996 testimony before the U.S. House Committee on International Relations and a Fulbright experience at the University of Michigan. These are documented elements of his public career, separate from later political controversies.

## The 2002 flood controversy

While deputy mayor, Gonxhja resigned amid a controversy over flooding in Tirana linked to the artificial lake. Contemporary reporting connected the resignation to the opening of a lake gate and a wider dispute over responsibility. [Radio Free Europe](https://www.evropaelire.org/a/1042366.html)

The available record supports the resignation and controversy. It does not establish the stronger versions sometimes repeated in political commentary—that he “fled,” was criminally prosecuted or caused a specific level of financial damage. Those claims require court or prosecution documents.

## ALBARENT and the ministerial appointment

ALBARENT Sh.p.k. operates in vehicle rental, passenger transport and related services. QKB-derived company records list Blendi Gonxhja as the original owner, Elda Gonxhja as administrator from 2010 and Elda as the current 100-percent owner.

The company reported turnover of 156.8 million ALL in 2021, 160.7 million ALL in 2022, 263.6 million ALL in 2023 and 165.7 million ALL in 2024. Its reported profit reached 49.8 million ALL in 2023. [Company record](https://opencorporates.al/sq/nipt/k92108006a)

On 11 January 2024, Blendi transferred the shares to Elda through a gift contract. On 12 January, he was appointed Minister of Economy, Culture and Innovation. The timing raises a conflict-of-interest question, but timing alone is not proof that the transfer was unlawful or designed to conceal ownership.

The registry also records a tax lien placed in October 2021 and released in November 2023. The existence of a lien does not establish tax evasion; the underlying tax decision and payment history would be needed.

## The incinerator link

The most politically sensitive corporate issue is the reported relationship between ALBARENT and Integrated Energy BV SPV, the company associated with the Tirana incinerator concession.

CNA and Boldnews reported that ALBARENT received approximately 151 million **old lek** during 2019–2021, with Boldnews describing more than 70 payments. That equals approximately 15.1 million modern ALL, or around €125,000–€130,000—not €1.26 million. Another report cited 73 million old lek during the first seven months of 2021. [Boldnews report](https://boldnews.al/2022/03/31/blendi-gonxhe-mori-150-mln-leke-nga-inceneratori-i-sharres-per-makina-me-qera/) · [CNA report](https://www.cna.al/politike/151-mln-leke-nga-integrated-energy-bv-spv-pd-kerkon-hetim-per-blendi-go-i374122) · [CNA report on 2021 payments](https://www.cna.al/denoncim/dokumenti-blendi-gonxhja-mori-rreth-73-milione-leke-nga-kompania-e-ince-i433200)

These reports create a documented reporting trail, not a proven criminal offence. The decisive documents would be invoices, contracts, bank transfers, procurement records and any SPAK or court findings. Elda Gonxhja's role as administrator during the reported period makes the relationship politically relevant, but the public record does not show that she personally “cashed checks” or that the payments were unlawful.

## What the family declarations show

The 2021 ILDKPKI declaration records Dante Gonxhja's business profit at 34.57 million ALL, approximately €288,000. The income section identifies the source only as “Fizik.” Dante was also associated with RentalCars Albania, a deregistered sole-trader business—not an LLC with conventional shares. [2021 declaration](https://spending.data.al/files/money_power/Blendi-Gonxhja-PVSD-2021%20%281%29.pdf) · [RentalCars registry record](https://opencorporates.al/sq/nipt/l41811018g)

David Gonxhja's 2024 declaration records a 67-square-metre apartment in the Netherlands valued at €350,000. The declared source is “income from parents.” The declaration confirms the asset and the stated source, but does not show the transfer agreement, bank trail or tax treatment. [2024 declaration](https://spending.data.al/files/money_power/Blendi-Gonxhja-PV-2024.pdf)

Elda Gonxhja's 2021 declaration also records a €155,576 deposit sourced to “sale of assets,” without identifying the assets sold. This is an unanswered documentation question, not proof of unexplained wealth.

## SPAK, promotion and the open-file problem

In July 2022, Nisma Thurje filed a complaint alleging abuse of office and tender violations at DPSHTRR. Gonxhja appeared at SPAK and was questioned for approximately one hour, according to contemporary reporting. [Euronews](https://euronews.al/blendi-gonxhe-paraqitet-ne-spak/) · [Complaint report](https://euronews.al/nisma-thurje-kallezon-ne-spak-blendi-gonxhen-per-abuzime-me-tenderet/)

Separate media reports have described broader allegations involving corruption, asset concealment and money laundering. In the sources reviewed for this profile, no official SPAK disposition was found confirming those claims or establishing their current status. Gonxhja was appointed minister in January 2024 and entered Parliament in 2025. Those promotions are documented political events; they are not proof that “nothing happened” in a legal sense. The accurate formulation is: **a complaint and appearance are documented; the outcome of any related proceeding remains unverified.**

## The 400-million-ALL Kanye West controversy

In July 2026, the government approved 400 million ALL in support for a Kanye West concert in Tirana. Gonxhja defended the decision and described the state intervention as support for a major international event. [Government announcement](https://www.kryeministria.al/en/newsroom/tirana-ne-fazen-finale-te-pergatitjeve-per-koncertin-e-madh-te-kanye-west/)

Critics have challenged the legal basis and public-interest rationale of using state funds for a private concert. The government’s action and Gonxhja’s public role are confirmed. Claims that the funding was illegal remain allegations unless established by an investigation or court decision.

## The NGO allegation

Pamfleti has alleged that Gonxhja was connected to the distribution of approximately €5.5 million to 201 party-linked NGOs. No official ministry grant database or primary award list was located to verify that claim. It should remain labeled as a partisan-media allegation, not a confirmed scandal. [Pamfleti report](https://pamfleti.net/english/politike/blendi-gonxhe-201-ojf-te-e-partise-me-55-milione-euro-ne-liste-dhe-ata--i225283)
