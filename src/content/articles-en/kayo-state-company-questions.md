On June 30, 2026, Prime Minister Edi Rama stood at the Pashaliman Naval Base in Vlora, unveiling Fincantieri Albania and promising 500 new jobs. The government presented KAYO sh.a., the state owned defence company created in October 2024, as Albania's defence industrial renaissance.

The public record presents a more complicated picture. KAYO was created with broad powers. Five joint ventures appeared in less than 100 days, with three established within three days of one another. Treasury records reviewed for this investigation show reported payments of EUR 23.4 million to TIMAK DEFENCE in under one year. MSSC appears in reported state contracts worth approximately EUR 59 million since 2018. A 49 percent stake in TIMAK changed hands for EUR 490 seven weeks before TIMAK became the private partner in a state defence joint venture.

The investigation examines whether the structure is a collection of separate events or one connected network. It does not claim that every person or entity named here has committed a crime. Claims based on media reports, unnamed sources, opposition statements or incomplete records are identified as claims and require further verification.

## Part 1: The network

### The Idrizi, Puca and Yeffet triangle

TIMAK, the company that holds 80 percent of TIMAK DEFENCE, entered Albania in September 2016 through the real estate agency of Sinan Idrizi, the Vlora businessman who controls MDN Investment, a 41 percent shareholder of Air Albania. Idrizi has attended joint meetings with Prime Minister Rama and President Erdogan.

On June 17, 2025, TIMAK owner Arjeta Puca sold 49 percent of the company to Ron Yeffet for EUR 490. The investigation says Yeffet and Idrizi were already co owners. They held shares together in MDN Investment in 2018, with Yeffet remaining for exactly 42 days before exiting. They also co owned Mifol Inert sh.pk, through which the investigation says gravel was extracted from the Vjosa River without permits for construction of the Vlora airport runway.

These records and reports place Idrizi, Puca and Yeffet in the same business cluster since at least 2016, using different corporate vehicles as new opportunities appeared. The relationship is a matter for documentary verification, not by itself proof of criminal conduct.

### The Veliu, Dusha, Ducka and McGonigal chain

Ardi Veliu served as State Police Director from 2018 to 2020. During that period, the police awarded at least nine negotiated contracts worth approximately EUR 3 million to MSSC, owned by Emiljano Dusha.

The investigation also reports that MSSC was used as a front company in State Police weapons tenders won by Dorjan Ducka. Ducka has been linked in public reporting to Charles McGonigal, the former FBI counterintelligence chief who was sentenced to 28 months in prison for concealing ties to Albanian businessmen and for multiple meetings with Rama. The police tenders were reportedly postponed three times before Ducka won them through MSSC.

Veliu now runs KAYO. Dusha's MSSC holds 80 percent of MKD, which is linked to ammunition production, and 49 percent of GUNA TACTICAL, which is linked to military uniforms. The same network therefore appears in the chain from police procurement to KAYO joint ventures and treasury payments.

The reported chain is: McGonigal, a convicted former FBI agent; Ducka, under reported SPAK and FBI scrutiny; MSSC and Dusha, reported to have been under SPAK investigation since August 2023; KAYO joint ventures; and the Albanian treasury.

### The Balluku and Veliu axis

Deputy Prime Minister Belinda Balluku signed government decisions that enabled KAYO's joint venture structure and the July 2025 amendments that made KAYO the mandatory first approach for defence procurement. She also signed the property transfer that gave KAYO the Kodër Babrru land where residents say an ammunition facility is being built close to homes.

Veliu served as Balluku's deputy minister at the Ministry of Energy and Infrastructure before he was installed as KAYO's administrator. Sot News described Balluku as the tutor who initiated the KAYO scheme and Veliu as her instrument. That description is a media characterization, not an established legal finding.

SPAK indicted Balluku in November 2025 in connection with allegations that she steered two construction tenders worth more than EUR 200 million. Rama dismissed her in February 2026. The investigation asks whether the person who helped create KAYO's legal framework should also be examined as part of its governance history.

### Every road leads to Rama

The investigation identifies Prime Minister Edi Rama as the political figure at the centre of the network. He appointed Veliu as police chief in 2018, then deputy minister, and later KAYO administrator. He promoted Puca on his personal podcast and visited her factory with ministers.

The report also describes Rama introducing fraud complainant Mark Faist to what Faist called his most impressive contacts, including Rama himself, during a private flight to Paris. Rama met McGonigal repeatedly. He promoted Ermal Nufi from KAYO board chairman to Defence Minister and signed government decisions with Balluku.

Yeffet has been described in reporting as Rama's personal friend and as someone who stayed at a government villa in the former Bllok. Former Finance Minister Arben Ahmetaj publicly stated that Rama asked him to sell Albania's public debt to Yeffet. These claims require the underlying court filings, official records and direct responses from the people involved.

## Part 2: The architecture

In October 2024, the government created KAYO sh.a., a fully state owned joint stock company under the Ministry of Defence. Its administrator is Ardi Veliu. The company was created with minimal capital, does not appear as a clear line in the 2026 State Budget, and has no published audit from the Supreme State Audit Institution, known as KLSH, in the materials reviewed for this investigation.

Law no. 88/2024 gives KAYO extraordinary powers:

1. KAYO can enter joint ventures with private partners through direct selection, without a competitive tender being required.
2. Security and defence structures must approach KAYO first for equipment and materials before launching public tenders. If KAYO cannot fulfil the request within 30 days, normal procurement may proceed. In practice, this makes KAYO the default channel for Albanian defence procurement.
3. State property can be transferred directly to KAYO by government decision.
4. Defence contracts can be classified under national security provisions, limiting ordinary public procurement transparency.

On July 16, 2025, amendments signed by Balluku expanded KAYO's authority and explicitly allowed the Ministry of Defence to use KAYO as a direct supply channel. The investigation argues that this bypassed domestic industry that previously competed in open tenders.

The result is a legal structure in which a state owned intermediary can receive the monopoly access, the treasury funding, the public property and the classification shield, while private partners receive most of the economic exposure in the joint ventures.

## Part 3: Three days, five joint ventures, EUR 105,000 in capital

Between August 1 and November 11, 2025, KAYO created five joint ventures. Three were founded within three days of one another. The combined initial capital across the first three companies was approximately EUR 105,000.

| Company | Founded | KAYO share | Private partner | Purpose | Capital |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- | --- | ---: |
| MKD | August 1, 2025 | 20% | MSSC, linked to Dusha, 80% | Ammunition production | EUR 35,000 |
| GUNA TACTICAL | August 1, 2025 | 51% | MSSC and TIMAK, 49% | Military uniforms | EUR 35,000 |
| TIMAK DEFENCE | August 4, 2025 | 20% | TIMAK, linked to Puca and Yeffet, 80% | Vehicles and anti drone systems | EUR 35,000 |
| ADVANCED ARMS TECHNOLOGY | November 11, 2025 | 20% | UNO18, linked to Yeffet, 80% | Firearms and drones | Not stated in the report |
| Fincantieri Albania | April 29, 2026 | 49% | Fincantieri SpA, 51% | Naval vessels | Pending |

In three of the four domestic joint ventures, KAYO holds only 20 percent while the private partner holds 80 percent. The state contributes monopoly access, treasury funding, property and legal protection. The investigation asks what the private partners contributed, particularly in the case of TIMAK, where a 49 percent stake was acquired for EUR 490 seven weeks before the state defence joint venture was created.

An opposition interpellation asked: “What is the real level of control of the Albanian state in these structures, when in three out of four cases the participation of KAYO is only 20 percent?”

## Part 4: Where the treasury goes

### TIMAK DEFENCE: EUR 23.4 million in under a year

Treasury data reviewed for the investigation shows five payments totalling 2,579,849,272 lek, approximately EUR 23.4 million, between October 2025 and June 2026.

#### Payment 1: Anti drone system

On September 26, 2025, the Defence and Security College decided to install a counter unmanned aircraft system, or C UAS. Around October 10, Military Department 3001 signed a contract with TIMAK DEFENCE. Three days later, the implementation order was issued. By the end of October, approximately EUR 6.1 million had been transferred.

The investigation found no corresponding trace in the ordinary public procurement bulletin.

#### Payment 2: Light tactical transport vehicles

On October 29, a secret contract was signed. On October 30, the Secretary General of the Ministry of Defence issued the monitoring order. On October 31, TIMAK DEFENCE issued an invoice for EUR 4.4 million. The payment was executed on November 4.

The elapsed time from contract to payment was six days. There was no public tender, no procurement bulletin entry, no published contract terms and no competitive process visible in the records reviewed. The money moved from the treasury through military departments into a company in which the Albanian state holds 20 percent and the private partner holds 80 percent.

The investigation quotes Hashtag.al comparing the speed of the coordination with the 17 decisions taken in one day in the incinerator scandal. The comparison is a media characterization.

### MSSC: EUR 59 million and a reported SPAK investigation

Open Spending Albania data reviewed for the investigation shows MSSC receiving approximately EUR 59 million in state contracts between 2018 and 2025, virtually all through direct negotiation without competitive tendering.

During Veliu's tenure as State Police Director, from July 2018 to December 2020, the police awarded nine negotiated contracts to MSSC worth approximately EUR 3 million. Between October and December 2024, the same months in which KAYO was created, the Ministry of Defence awarded seven more negotiated contracts to MSSC worth approximately EUR 6 million.

MSSC's reported financial trajectory also changed sharply. The company grew from modest capital to 1 billion lek, approximately EUR 9 million, by 2024. It declared 822 million lek in profit before tax in 2023 and 473 million lek in 2024. The investigation says these profits came almost entirely from state contracts.

MSSC has reportedly been under SPAK investigation since August 2023 in relation to corruption in arms tenders and weapons allegedly sold to the army. This is a reported investigative status, not a conviction.

The investigation therefore asks why a company described as a weapons trader and importer, with no established manufacturing experience, was selected as the exclusive partner for military uniforms and ammunition manufacturing.

### GUNA TACTICAL: the uniform monopoly

The July 2025 amendments redirected military uniform procurement through KAYO and GUNA TACTICAL, according to the investigation. This removed the domestic garment industry from competitive tenders and made the annual military clothing budget flow through the joint venture.

GUNA TACTICAL was created with approximately EUR 35,000 in capital. Neither KAYO nor MSSC is identified in the investigation as having experience in textile production. Critics have argued that the operation could buy uniforms from Turkey or China and resell them as Made in Albania at inflated prices. There have also been complaints that uniforms supplied to date were of poor quality. Those claims require procurement documents, product testing and supplier records.

### The Kodër Babrru deception claim

In February 2026, residents of Kodër Babrru in Tirana said they discovered that KAYO was building an ammunition factory and weapons dismantling unit five metres from their homes. The investigation says regulations require a minimum 1.5 kilometre safety buffer.

The building permit information board identifies the project as the construction of a two floor textile factory. The developer is KAYO and the contractor is MSSC Group sh.p.k.

The Dervishi family's private property, plots 173 and 174 in the cadastre, was transferred to the Ministry of Defence for KAYO without public notice, explanation or consent, according to the investigation. The transfer was signed by Balluku. The land is now surrounded by a gate and guarded by soldiers, while the owners are reportedly prohibited from entering their own property.

Residents have compared the situation to Gerdec, the 2008 ammunition depot explosion that killed 26 people. They have filed criminal complaints and threatened to take the case to the European Court of Human Rights.

The central question is simple: if the project is a textile factory, where are the plans and production records? If it is an ammunition facility, where are the safety approvals, the buffer-zone analysis and the legal basis for the location?

### The Elbit sovereign guarantee

The Elbit Systems contract has been reported at between EUR 400 million and EUR 1 billion. The reported financing model is that Elbit finds bank financing, Albania provides a sovereign guarantee, and Albanian taxpayers assume repayment including interest.

The investigation says Elbit was suspended by NATO's procurement agency on July 31, 2025 for corruption, around the same time Albania was signing its agreement with Elbit. The underlying suspension decision and the government's due diligence file should be published before this allegation is treated as established fact.

Ron Yeffet is described in the investigation as the broker of the deal. Elbit registered an Albanian branch, Elbit Systems Ltd. Dega ne Shqiperi, with NIPT M61712022Q on May 12, 2026. The VKM approving the Albania Israel defence capabilities framework agreement is dated September 10, 2025, six weeks after the reported NSPA suspension.

The report says the financing scheme was discussed as a factor in Defence Minister Pirro Vengu's dismissal and replacement by Ermal Nufi, who was described in Ministry corridors as someone willing to sign anything asked of him. That is an attributed claim requiring official correspondence or a direct response.

### No budget line

Seven opposition MPs asked how a state owned company of such strategic importance could have no line in the 2026 State Budget and no clear place in the government's medium term strategy.

KAYO does not appear as a clear line item in the budget reviewed for the investigation. The money reaches KAYO and its joint ventures through military department transfers, classified contracts and direct negotiation procurement. The report argues that these channels operate below the level of ordinary parliamentary budget scrutiny.

## Part 5: The people

### KAYO sh.a.: the state vehicle

Administrator: Ardi Veliu, former State Police Director from 2018 to 2020 and former Deputy Minister under Balluku.

The supervisory board has been identified as including Ida Malile, Gjergji Methoxha, Ermal Nufi, Aleks Marku, Ervin Feti and Grestian Poci. Nufi was the former chairman and later became Defence Minister, the minister whose institution is KAYO's sole shareholder.

### Ron Yeffet: the broker

Ron Yeffet is described as an Israeli Albanian businessman. He holds 49 percent of TIMAK and 100 percent of UNO18, giving him reported exposure to TIMAK DEFENCE and ADVANCED ARMS TECHNOLOGY. The investigation also describes him as a broker of the reported Elbit deal.

Yeffet is being sued in Miami Dade County by Mark Faist for USD 8.6 million in fraud. The lawsuit describes introductions to Rama on private jets and at Versailles before money was taken from the plaintiff. A settlement agreement signed on September 29, 2025 acknowledged a debt of USD 7,611,676.50, of which only USD 1 million was paid, according to the investigation.

In a counterclaim, Yeffet stated that he was removed from an unnamed Albanian development project worth more than USD 30 million because of media coverage of his connections to Rama. His lawyers sought discovery of documents related to “Albanian Development” and the investor group. The court filings should establish whether the project was connected to the Albanian state.

The investigation also reports that Yeffet is being sued by the Republic of Togo over the seizure of its United Nations embassy headquarters in New York through 40th Development LLC. It reports an investigation in Senegal into coastal land deals and approximately EUR 55 million in construction contracts. These are matters to be verified against court dockets and official records.

The report connects Yeffet to Asaf Barazani, described as the owner of Casino Vlora and as someone suspected of links to the Milton call centre fraud group under Europol and Eurojust investigation. It also identifies Yeffet's previous Albanian ventures: a 42 day co ownership of MDN Investment with Sinan Idrizi in 2018, co ownership of Mifol Inert with Idrizi, and an attempt to privatise the Albanian embassy in Washington, DC that was blocked by Foreign Minister Ditmir Bushati.

### Emiljano Dusha and MSSC: the arms trader turned defence partner

Emiljano Dusha owns MSSC, founded in 2009. MSSC holds 80 percent of MKD and 49 percent of GUNA TACTICAL. The company received approximately EUR 59 million in reported state contracts since 2018, largely without competitive tendering. Its capital grew to 1 billion lek by 2024, with reported profit before tax of 822 million lek in 2023 and 473 million lek in 2024.

MSSC has reportedly been under SPAK investigation since August 2023 for corruption in arms tenders and for trafficking weapons sold to the army. The investigation says MSSC was used as a front company in State Police tenders won by Dorjan Ducka, who is linked in public reporting to McGonigal.

Albanian media have also described Dusha as connected to vote buying operations on behalf of the ruling majority and violence against journalists since the late 1990s. These are serious allegations that require attribution and primary documentation.

MSSC is identified as the contractor building the Kodër Babrru facility that appears on the building permit as a textile factory. Sot News described Dusha as an old client of Veliu. Timoni.al called him a trafficker who had served the party for votes and was later declared a state partner to supply ammunition and uniforms. Those are media descriptions, not judicial findings.

### Ardi Veliu: the operational hub

Veliu was State Police Director under Rama from July 2018 to December 2020, then Deputy Minister of Energy and Infrastructure under Balluku, and is now KAYO's administrator. During his police tenure, MSSC received nine negotiated State Police contracts.

His career path is therefore police chief, deputy minister, and CEO of a state arms company that entered joint ventures with the same private company that received contracts during his police tenure. Sot News described him as having transformed himself into a magician of a corrupt market in the military industry. That description is a media allegation.

### Arjeta Puca: the gatekeeper

Puca is administrator of TIMAK DEFENCE and ADVANCED ARMS TECHNOLOGY, two KAYO joint ventures in different defence categories. She sold 49 percent of TIMAK to Yeffet for EUR 490 on June 17, 2025, seven weeks before TIMAK entered a state defence joint venture.

TIMAK entered Albania in 2016 through Idrizi's real estate agency. Pamfleti described Puca as the mask of the Idrizi and Babloku network. In March 2023, the government gave TIMAK 30,000 square metres of free state premises at the former Metalurgjiku in Elbasan. Rama personally promoted TIMAK on his podcast.

Pamfleti has alleged that TIMAK is linked to the Turkish company TIMAC and to the Erdogan family. Puca lived in Turkey for 25 years. The SHOTA military vehicle, presented as Made in Albania, is alleged to be assembled from Turkish sourced parts. NATO reportedly rejected Rama's pitch to purchase EUR 35 million worth of SHOTA vehicles for Albania, Macedonia, Montenegro and Kosovo.

Confidential Ministry of Defence sources cited by Pamfleti alleged that the original plan was for TIMAK to acquire 60 percent of KAYO, with the government retaining only 15 percent and the remaining 25 percent hidden in offshore structures. This claim requires corporate documents and should not be presented as established fact.

### Ermal Nufi: the revolving door

Nufi served as chairman of KAYO's board during 2025, when all five joint ventures were created. He collected income from the KAYO board and four other state boards, including Durres Yacht and Marina, Albpetrol, the Cadastre Agency and the Albanian Road Authority. The investigation calculates more than 3.7 million lek in board fees in 2025 alone.

Nufi was appointed Defence Minister in March 2026. The person who oversaw the creation of KAYO's joint ventures then became the minister responsible for the Ministry that owns KAYO.

When asked about the DronesVision drone partnership, Nufi described DronesVision as a Thai company with more than 20 years of experience. The investigation says DronesVision is actually a Taiwanese company based in Keelung City and founded in 2010. The discrepancy raises a question about the accuracy of the Ministry's description of its partner.

### Sinan Idrizi: the connector

Idrizi is not formally part of KAYO, but the investigation identifies him as the person connecting Puca, Yeffet and Rama. He created MDN Investment in May 2018 for the Vlora Airport concession and brought Yeffet in as co owner for 42 days.

MDN held 41 percent of Air Albania, alongside Turkish Airlines with 49 percent and Albcontrol with 10 percent. Air Albania is now reported to be carrying significant debt, with US and Ukrainian companies suing over unpaid aircraft leases and a catering firm owed EUR 5 million.

The investigation reports that Idrizi's wife Najada and daughter Rejana, the 100 percent owners of MDN Investment, fled to Turkey to escape criminal lawsuits. As of May 2026, reports said that the Idrizi and Yeffet network was attempting to take the Vlora Airport concession from current concessionaire Behgjet Pacolli and redirect it through MDN Investment.

### Belinda Balluku: the legal architect

Balluku was Deputy Prime Minister and Infrastructure Minister when she signed the decisions creating the KAYO joint venture framework, the July 2025 amendments establishing KAYO's procurement role, and the Kodër Babrru property transfer.

SPAK indicted her in November 2025 over allegations involving two construction tenders worth more than EUR 200 million. Rama dismissed her in February 2026. Her legal status is separate from the KAYO decisions she signed, and the investigation does not treat one as automatic proof of the other.

### Elbit Systems: the sovereign guarantee

Elbit Systems registered an Albanian branch as Elbit Systems Ltd. Dega ne Shqiperi, NIPT M61712022Q, on May 12, 2026. The administrator is Danjela Xhako. The registry identifies Michael Ilan Yoel Federmann, an Israeli citizen born in 1943, as the ultimate beneficial owner through Federmann Enterprises, with a reported 33.3 percent holding.

The branch is connected to VKM no. 496 dated September 10, 2025, concerning the Albania Israel defence capabilities framework agreement. The investigation describes the agreement as being signed six weeks after the reported NATO procurement suspension and asks why the government has not published its due diligence.

## Part 6: The property transfers

KAYO has used property transfer powers in at least four cases identified in the investigation.

### Former Autotraktor plant, Shkoze

The former Autotraktor plant was transferred to KAYO by government decision. On August 9, 2025, five days after the first joint ventures were created, State Police surrounded the building at 7 a.m. on a Saturday, cut electricity and barred News24 journalists from entering. The action forced Albania's largest 24 hour news channel off air.

Approximately 230 media workers across News24, BalkanWeb, Panorama and Gazeta Shqiptare were affected. Journalists reported that investigative files on corruption were forcibly removed from their computers. The OSCE, EU Delegation, Reporters Without Borders, SafeJournalists Network and BIRN issued condemnations. News24 had a contract reportedly valid until 2033.

### Kodër Babrru, Tirana

The Dervishi family's private property, plots 173 and 174, was transferred to the Ministry of Defence for KAYO without consent, according to the investigation. The land is now associated with the disputed ammunition facility and is guarded by armed soldiers. The owners are reportedly barred from entering.

### Former Metalurgjiku, Elbasan

In March 2023, the government gave TIMAK 30,000 square metres of free state premises at the former Metalurgjiku site in Elbasan.

### Rubik factory

The Rubik factory is being transformed into a weapons production centre. Rama, former Defence Minister Pirro Vengu and Justice Minister Ulsi Manja visited the premises together, according to the investigation.

The report argues that the same mechanism appears in each case: a government decision, no public tender, no clear public compensation, and armed security used to enforce access or transfer.

## Part 7: The shield

KAYO operates behind several layers of opacity.

### Classification

Defence contracts can be classified under national security provisions. Contract values, terms and partner selection criteria are therefore not ordinarily published. The Elbit contract has been reported at figures ranging from EUR 400 million to EUR 1 billion, while the government has not confirmed a number in the materials reviewed.

### No competitive tender

Law no. 88/2024 allows direct selection of partners. That means no ordinary procurement bulletin entry, no competing bids and no published evaluation criteria. The C UAS contract that produced the first reported EUR 6.1 million payment to TIMAK DEFENCE left no trace in the ordinary public procurement bulletin.

### No budget line

KAYO does not appear as a clear line item in the State Budget, so Parliament does not vote on its funding in the same way it votes on ordinary budget lines.

### No published audit

No KLSH audit of KAYO has been published in the materials reviewed for this investigation.

### The supervisory council

The supervisory council includes representatives from the Prime Minister's Office, Defence Intelligence and the General Staff of the Armed Forces. Its chairman during the joint venture creation period was Ermal Nufi, who then became Defence Minister. The investigation asks whether a supervisory structure can provide independent oversight when its chairman later assumes direct ministerial responsibility for the same state company.

### Building permit questions

At Kodër Babrru, a project described by residents as an ammunition factory is identified on the building permit as a textile factory. If the government is using a different official description for the project, the public needs to know why and which permit governs the safety regime.

## Part 8: More sophisticated than the incinerators

Albanian media and opposition politicians have drawn parallels between KAYO, the incinerator scandal, infrastructure concessions and tunnel contracts. The investigation argues that KAYO's structure is more sophisticated because it uses a state owned company as a pass through.

Treasury payments can appear to go to a state entity rather than directly to private companies. Joint ventures can leave the state with 20 percent while the private partner receives 80 percent of the economic exposure. The state supplies the monopoly, the property, the legal protection and the funding.

Contracts can be classified under national security provisions, placing them beyond ordinary freedom of information requests, procurement transparency and journalistic scrutiny. Property powers can be used against independent media, private landowners and domestic garment manufacturers excluded from uniform tenders. The investigation says armed police and military were used without court orders in some of these disputes.

The structure is designed, the report argues, so that every ordinary control mechanism, including competitive tendering, budget oversight, audit, independent supervision and freedom of information, either does not apply, has been bypassed, or is staffed by people connected to the arrangement.

## Part 9: The questions that remain

These are not rhetorical questions. They should have documented answers in government files.

1. Where are the partner selection reports? Law no. 88/2024 requires legal compliance and conflict of interest controls. Did KAYO run an adverse media or litigation check on Ron Yeffet before approving joint ventures that give him indirect exposure to tens of millions in state funds? Did anyone check that Emiljano Dusha had reportedly been under SPAK investigation since August 2023? If so, publish the reports.

2. Where is the due diligence on Elbit Systems? Was the government aware of the reported NSPA corruption suspension when it continued the relationship? The VKM approving the Albania Israel framework agreement is dated September 10, 2025, six weeks after the reported July 31 suspension. What checks were performed?

3. What is the total value of treasury payments to all KAYO joint ventures? TIMAK DEFENCE alone has received approximately EUR 23.4 million. What about MKD, GUNA TACTICAL and ADVANCED ARMS TECHNOLOGY?

4. What is the sovereign guarantee amount for the Elbit contract? What is the principal, the interest rate and the total repayment obligation for Albanian taxpayers?

5. What is the USD 30 million Albanian project that Yeffet says he lost? His lawyers told a Miami court that he was removed from an Albanian real estate development investor group. Which project was it, and was it connected to the state?

6. Why was there no budget line for KAYO in the 2026 State Budget?

7. Why was News24 evicted? What was the legal basis? Why did KAYO need the former Autotraktor plant urgently enough to deploy armed police against a broadcaster with a reported contract valid until 2033?

8. What is the real nature of the Kodër Babrru construction? The building permit says textile factory. Residents say ammunition factory. Which is it? If it is an ammunition facility, why does the permit describe something else?

9. Why is MSSC Group, a company reported to be under investigation for arms corruption, the contractor for state defence construction projects?

10. Who are the beneficial owners behind the offshore structures that Pamfleti sources say were planned to hold 25 percent of KAYO?

## The bottom line

The investigation's conclusion is that KAYO is not simply a defence company. It describes KAYO as a legal architecture for converting public money into private wealth, operated by an interconnected network appearing across companies, sectors and earlier controversies, from Air Albania to Vlora Airport, the Vjosa River and the defence industry.

The alleged network uses classification as a shield, monopoly as a funnel, minority state ownership as a fig leaf and armed force as enforcement. The Albanian treasury provides the money. The money moves through military departments into joint ventures where private partners often hold 80 percent. One private partner is a businessman facing fraud litigation in several jurisdictions who acquired his TIMAK stake for EUR 490. Another is a weapons importer reported to be under SPAK investigation whose companies received approximately EUR 59 million in negotiated state contracts over seven years.

The person who helped build the legal framework was later indicted by SPAK. The supervisory structures were staffed by people involved in creating the arrangements. The contracts were classified, making the financial structure difficult to see.

This is the question the documents leave behind: was KAYO created to build a defence industry, or was the defence industry used to build a system in which public money could move without public scrutiny?

We are requesting the full company extracts, Council of Ministers decisions, partner selection files, due diligence reports, treasury rows, contracts, invoices, financing terms, property documents, building permits, audit records and a right of reply from every person and entity named in this investigation.
