On June 30, 2026, Prime Minister Edi Rama appeared at the Pashaliman Naval Base in Vlora as the government presented a new chapter for Albania's defence industry. The project was presented alongside the name Fincantieri Albania.

The public story is an industrial renaissance. The documents tell a more complicated story: a state company created with broad powers, four domestic joint ventures formed in a matter of months, public property placed in its orbit, and treasury payments that are difficult to follow from the outside.

This investigation does not claim that every person named here committed a crime. It asks a narrower question, one that can be answered with records: how did Albania build a system in which the state provides the legal channel, the property and the access to public money, while private partners receive the economic exposure?

## The company at the centre

KAYO sh.a. was created under Law no. 88/2024 and placed under the Ministry of Defence. The public registry identifies the Ministry as the sole owner and shareholder. The registered administrator is Ardi Veliu, who previously served as Director of the State Police and later as a deputy minister.

The law gives KAYO a wide mandate. It can design, produce, repair, demilitarise, dispose of and trade weapons, ammunition, military equipment, military technology and related services. It can receive state property and obtain financing with government approval.

That is not a scandal by itself. It is a concentration of power that requires unusually strong oversight. KAYO can operate as producer, agent, contracting authority and private sector partner. When one state company wears that many hats, the public needs to know who is checking the wardrobe.

## Five joint ventures, four that are documented

The public KAYO registry identifies four domestic joint ventures.

TIMAK DEFENCE was established with TIMAK holding 80 percent and KAYO holding 20 percent. M.K.D. was established with MSSC holding 80 percent and KAYO holding 20 percent. GUNA TACTICAL gives KAYO 51 percent and MSSC 49 percent. ADVANCED ARMS TECHNOLOGY gives UNO18 80 percent and KAYO 20 percent.

The working investigation also refers to Fincantieri Albania, described as a naval venture involving the Italian shipbuilder. That arrangement requires a separate publication of the Albanian company extract, the agreement, the site plan and the financing documents. Until those records are available, it remains a lead under verification.

The question is not whether a state company may work with private companies. It is whether the public can see how the partners were selected, what each side contributed, who performed the checks and who carries the loss when a project fails.

## The TIMAK timeline

The TIMAK records create a timeline that deserves attention.

On June 17, 2025, Arjeta Puca transferred 49 percent of TIMAK to Ron Yeffet for 49,000 lek. On July 17, the company expanded its business activity into defence. On August 4, TIMAK DEFENCE was established, with TIMAK holding 80 percent and KAYO 20 percent.

Those are registry facts. They are not proof of wrongdoing. They are, however, the reason a partner selection file matters. Law no. 88/2024 refers to reliability, a clean record and reputation as criteria for private partners. Did KAYO prepare due diligence reports? If the reports exist, why have they not been published in a form that protects legitimate security information while still showing the public how the decision was made?

## Follow the money

The working investigation calculates five treasury payments to TIMAK DEFENCE between October 2025 and June 2026, totalling 2,579,849,272 lek, or approximately 23.4 million euros.

The reported payments include an anti drone system and light tactical transport vehicles. The investigation says the contracts and payments moved in a matter of days and left no visible trace in the ordinary public procurement bulletin.

That figure should be published with the underlying treasury rows, contracts, invoices and procurement purpose attached. Until that evidence is archived row by row, 23.4 million euros is a reported working finding, not a final conclusion.

The same investigation places MSSC at approximately 59 million euros in state contracts between 2018 and 2025. It also identifies nine negotiated State Police contracts worth approximately 3 million euros during Veliu's period as police director, and further Ministry of Defence contracts in 2024.

These numbers matter because MSSC is the private partner in two KAYO ventures. The public record establishes the corporate structure. Claims about corruption or trafficking connected to MSSC must be tied to a specific SPAK file, court document or clearly attributed report.

## The Babru question

VKM no. 410, dated July 16, 2025, placed properties 173 and 174 in Babru, Tirana, at KAYO's disposal.

The investigation file raises a serious question about the project on that land. Residents say the site is connected to ammunition production or weapons dismantling, while the building permit information is described as a textile factory. That is not a detail to be settled by political messaging. It requires the permit, technical design, safety assessment, inspection records and an explanation of the property's ownership history.

If the project is a textile factory, publish the plans. If it is an ammunition facility, publish the safety rules and the legal basis for the location. The public should not have to infer the nature of a strategic state project from the height of a gate.

## The Elbit file

Elbit Systems Ltd has an Albanian branch in the public registry, with NIPT M61712022Q. The registry links the branch to VKM no. 496, dated September 10, 2025, concerning an Albania Israel defence capabilities framework agreement.

The investigation file reports a financing model involving a sovereign guarantee and an arms contract whose value has been reported between 400 million euros and 1 billion euros. The government has not publicly established the principal, interest rate, repayment schedule or total taxpayer exposure in the materials reviewed for this article.

That is the central question. A sovereign guarantee is not a press release. It is a future obligation for the public budget. Before anyone celebrates a defence deal, the public should know what the state guarantees, to whom, and under what conditions.

The file also links the agreement to claims about an international procurement suspension involving Elbit. Those claims require the underlying NATO procurement decision and the government's due diligence file. They should not be turned into a verdict by headline alone.

## The political chain

The investigation maps a series of public appointments and decisions around KAYO. Veliu moved from State Police Director to deputy minister and then to KAYO administrator. Ermal Nufi chaired the KAYO supervisory board during the period when the joint ventures were created and later became Defence Minister.

The file also contains attributed claims that Defence Minister Pirro Vengu was removed after refusing to sign an arms agreement and that Nufi was appointed as a more willing signatory. Those claims come from unnamed sources and require official correspondence, cabinet records or a direct response from the people involved.

Belinda Balluku's name appears in the investigation because she signed government decisions connected to KAYO's property and procurement framework. The file also describes her separate legal exposure in a SPAK case. Those are separate matters. A person's legal status must be described precisely, and a connection to a government decision is not proof that the decision was unlawful.

## What the law hides and what it does not

Law no. 88/2024 includes classified, commercial and industrial secrecy provisions for some KAYO activities and partner selection procedures. The investigation argues that the secrecy rules can block ordinary public scrutiny of contract values, evaluation criteria and partner records.

National security can justify withholding sensitive operational details. It cannot automatically justify withholding the existence of a contract, the identity of the contracting parties, the value of public guarantees, the use of public land or the existence of a conflict of interest review.

The difference matters. A defence secret protects a capability. It should not become a blanket label for every financial and governance document around that capability.

## What is established and what is still a claim

The public record establishes that KAYO is state owned, that the Ministry of Defence controls it, that it has broad legal powers, that it has entered domestic joint ventures, and that government decisions have placed public property and negotiations within its structure.

The investigation raises additional claims about rigged tenders, trafficking, hidden ownership, ministerial dismissals, classified payments, foreign connections, property seizures and a single coordinated network. Those claims may be important. They require separate evidence and should not be presented as proven simply because they fit a compelling narrative.

This is where journalism has to be less cinematic than the story. The strongest article is not the one with the most explosive adjective. It is the one that leaves every serious reader with a document they can open and a question the authorities cannot answer with a slogan.

## The questions that remain

Where are the KAYO partner selection reports?

What is the full value of treasury payments to every KAYO joint venture?

What is the principal and total repayment obligation under any Elbit sovereign guarantee?

What exactly is being built at Babru, and under which safety permit?

Why is the 2026 state budget not clearly showing KAYO's financing structure?

What records explain the transfer and use of properties connected to KAYO?

Who selected the private partners, and what due diligence was completed before public money began to move?

KAYO may become a genuine defence industry. It may also become a lesson in how a public company can be given so much authority that the public can no longer follow the money. The difference will be decided by the documents.

We are requesting the full company extracts, Council of Ministers decisions, partner selection files, due diligence reports, treasury rows, contracts, invoices, financing terms, property documents, building permits and a right of reply for every entity mentioned.
