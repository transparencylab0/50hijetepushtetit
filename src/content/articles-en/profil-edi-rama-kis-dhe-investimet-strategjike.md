This profile is not a political biography and it is not a judgment of guilt. It is a working page for understanding an institutional role: when the Prime Minister leads or is linked to strategic-investment decision-making, which documents should be followed?

## The role that matters publicly

In strategic-investment files, the decision chain matters: the applicant, the institution reviewing the file, the committee approving it, the conditions imposed and the benefits or obligations each side receives.

For a political profile, the useful question is not simply "who said it." The more useful question is: **which document supports the decision, what condition does it impose and who answers publicly for it?**

## What should be followed

- Decisions mentioning the Strategic Investment Committee.
- Projects where land, water, coastline or public property enter a relationship with a private investor.
- Cases where political decisions are accompanied by missing public documents.
- Replies, clarifications or objections from institutions and mentioned parties.

## Editorial boundary

Profiling should not become a list of accusations. A useful profile links public role to documents, dates, decisions and readable sources. When a document is missing, the page should say clearly that the question remains open.

That is the boundary that keeps the archive useful: less noise, more evidence.
